The Life of Adrian

Personal

I am married and have four beautiful children - three girls and a boy. My oldest, the girls, are from a previous marriage and live with their mother in Florida; their ages: 17, 13, and 11. My son is 8 years old.

I was born in New York and moved around a great deal as a child; by the time I earned my undergraduate degree at age 21, I had moved 20 times, but was mostly raised in South Florida.

Education

Bachelors of Science in Computer Science, Northeastern University: 1986. Northeastern was the birthplace of cooperative education in 1908; Co-Op assignments were required but stretched the degree timeframe out over five years instead of the normal four. I spent all of my co-op assignments at IBM in various locations: White Plains, NY, Boca Raton, FL, Cambridge, MA. I highly recommend co-op programs to others. For me, I know that I hit the ground running when I joined IBM out of college.

MBA, The University of Texas: 1999. I got a bit frustrated in my career at one point - working on OS/2 and other IBM products that failed in the marketplace. I realized, however, that I did not have the tools to understand whether or not a product was destined for success. For this reason, I went to business school to earn an MBA. The executive program is geared towards allowing professionals to keep working and attend biweekly classes on Friday/Saturday. It sounds lovely, until you dig more deeply into the project assignments that required between 20 and 30 hours of work a week. My intention was not necessarily to get into management, but to understand business. Alas, it lead me out of software development and towards more managerial responsibilities.

Work

  • IBM - 13 years: mostly operating system development, some middleware development - Poughkeepsie, NY, Boca Raton, FL, Austin, TX
  • Dell - 3+ years: architecture, training, PMO, Chief of Staff for VP, Manager
  • Contracting with IBM from home - back to OS development
  • Brief stint with a consulting firm in Fairfax, VA
  • ThoughtWorks: five years beginning in 2005, where I cut my teeth on agile - as a developer, iteration manager/scrumMaster, project lead, delivery principal.
  • Bayview Financial: an eight-month stint in the commercial mortgage business as a software development director for a team of 45 in Coral Gables, FL in the center of the 2008 mortgage/banking industry collapse. Guess I needn't point out, that didn't last.
  • Lab49: Summer/fall of 2011 - agile coaching in the financial services and investment banking space.
  • Coriander Technologies: A handful of independent agile coaching/consulting gigs in various locations in the insurance and financial services domains.
  • CDX: Development Manager for Cox Digital Exchange - a startup in the B2B space for car dealerships - part of the Cox Enterprises family of companies. I've been here since fall, 2012.
