The Life of Adrian

Personal

I am married and have four beautiful children - three girls and a boy. My oldest, the girls, are from a previous marriage and live with their mother in Florida; their ages: 17, 13, and 11. My son is 8 years old.

I was born in New York and moved around a great deal as a child; by the time I earned my undergraduate degree at age 21, I had moved 20 times, but was mostly raised in South Florida.

Education

I earned a Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science from Northeastern University in 1986. Northeastern was the birthplace of cooperative education in 1908; Co-Op assignments were required but stretched the degree timeframe out over five years instead of the normal four. I spent all of my co-op assignments at IBM in various locations: White Plains, NY, Boca Raton, FL, Cambridge, MA. I highly recommend co-op programs to others. For me, I know that I hit the ground running when I joined IBM out of college.

I got a bit frustrated in my career at one point - working on OS/2 and other IBM products that failed in the marketplace. I realized, however, that I did not have the tools to understand whether or not a product was destined for success. For this reason, I went to business school to earn an MBA. I earned an MBA from The University of Texas through the Executive MBA program in 1999. The executive program is geared towards allowing professionals to keep working and attend biweekly classes on Friday/Saturday. It sounds lovely, until you dig more deeply into the project assignments that required between 20 and 30 hours of work a week. My intention was not necessarily to get into management, but to understand business. Alas, it lead me out of software development and towards more managerial responsibilities.

Work

  • IBM - 13 years: mostly operating system development, some middleware development - Poughkeepsie, NY, Boca Raton, FL, Austin, TX
  • Dell - 3+ years: architecture, training, PMO, Chief of Staff for VP, Manager
  • Contracting with IBM from home - back to OS development
  • Brief 3-month stint with a consulting firm in Fairfax, VA
  • Five years at ThoughtWorks, beginning in 2005, where I cut my teeth on agile - as a developer, iteration manager/scrumMaster, project lead, delivery principal.
  • An eight-month stint in the commercial mortgage domain as a software development director for a team of 45 - at Bayview Financial in Coral Gables, FL in the heart of the 2008 mortgage/banking industry collapse. Guess I should point out, that didn't last.
  • A five-month stint at Lab49 in Manhattan - doing agile coaching in the financial services and investment banking space.
  • A handful of independent agile coaching/consulting gigs in various locations
  • Development Manager for Cox Digital Exchange - a startup in the B2B space for car dealerships - part of the Cox Enterprises family of companies.
